Layout Rules

1 - collapsing margins

I'm just going to cover the very basic concept of collapsing margins here

For a more comprehensive explanation, checkout this article from howtocreate.co.uk and the w3 specifications

When two block level elements of the same block formatting context adjoin vertically (ie are above one another) and they both have a margin that meet, the smallest margin is collapsed (set to 0) so the space between them is equal to the largest of the two margins.

collapsing margins in action

Lets create two div elements. both will have a height of 18px (the height of our guide lines).
the first will have a margin of 18px (1 guide line).
the second will have a margin of 36px (2 guide lines). 

    .div1, .div2 {
      height: 18px;
    }
    .div1 {
      background-color: blue;
      margin: 18px;
    }
    .div2 {
      background-color: green;
      margin: 36px;
    }

    <div>div ------------------------ no padding or margin</div>
    <div class="div1"></div>
    <div class="div2"></div>
    <div>div ------------------------ no padding or margin</div>
div ------------------------ no padding or margin
div ------------------------ no padding or margin



here we can see that the blue div has a margin of 18px (1 guide line) above it but below there are 2 guide lines (26px). That's the margin from the bottom div.
As 26 > 18 we are only using the bottom div's margin. The margin from the top div has been 'collapsed' ie removed.
